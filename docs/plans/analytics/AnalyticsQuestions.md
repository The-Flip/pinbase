# Potential questions to be answered by analytics

Not all of these may be questions to be answered by [analytics](Analytics.md).

## Root questions

The subsequent sections try to answer these root questions in various ways.

- Are we growing?
- What's driving our growth?
  - What external traffic sources
  - What content of ours
- Are our theses about why people will like our site being borne out? See [Overview.md](../../Overview.md) and [Personas.md](../../Personas.md)
- Is our editor community healthy? Engaged? Growing?
- Do we have the right features?
  - Are our features being used?
  - Are some features missing the mark / frustrating users?
  - Are we missing features?

## Reach & discovery

- Is reach growing month over month?
- Where are visitors coming from — Google, social, direct, inbound links from pinball forums?
- Are referring domains diversifying, or is one source carrying us?
- Which search engine queries land people on the site?

## Usage of specific features

How much are these features used? Do the people that use them use them frequently?

- Claim revert
- View edit history
- View sources
- Clicking the edit link on accordions
- User contribution profile page
- the list goes on
- Titles page
- Titles page search
- Titles page filtering
- Manufacturers page
- People page
- Search on the various sub pages, meaning any search other than global search
- Global search
- Do people actually look at our privacy and term? Api docs?

## What people find vs. don't

- A list of the most common on-site searches and which don't return results (= prioritized catalog gap list)
- When a search has results, do people click one?
- Which pages get visited via search vs. via direct navigation vs. via links from other pages on the site?
- Are people coming to look at specific models (Pro, Premium, LE etc), or are they looking for titles? A thesis of ours is that the title-first market is under-served. See [Overview.md § Why The Domain Model Is A Differentiator](../../Overview.md#why-the-domain-model-is-a-differentiator).

## Content shape

- How deep do people go — one page and gone, or browse multiple? One of our theses is that we can build a more engaging and informative way of browsing pinball information. Did we succeed?
- Which titles/models get the most pageviews?
- Which pages get visited but have no real content (stub records)?
- Is anyone looking at pages we put a lot of work into?

## Contribution funnel

- How many anonymous visitors ever try to do something that requires login?
- How many signups complete vs. abandon partway?
- How many new accounts make their first edit? Within how long?
- How many people start an edit and don't save?
- How many media uploads start vs. complete?
- Of contributors who make one edit, how many make a second within 30 days?

## Community / retention

- How many contributors are active in a given month?
- Of new contributors from a given week, how many are still active 3 months later?
- Is the contributor base growing, flat, or churning?
- How many total edits this week vs last week, this month vs last month, and eventually this year vs last year
- How many contributors made an edit this week vs last week, this month vs last month, and eventually this year vs last year
- What's the profile of our editors? Do they make hundreds of edits or just a few? Like, some graph of the # of editors this day/week/month that made the most edits. I expect to see some sort of 80/20 rule where a small handful of very committed editors are making 100's of edits a week, while most make 1 or 2.
